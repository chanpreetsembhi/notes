Written by William Wordsworth, a well-known poet of the Romantic age, _The Preface to The Lyrical Ballads_ works like a guide for Romantic poetry. In it, Wordsworth explains his new idea of poetry. The poems were written by him and his friend Samuel Coleridge. He says that poetry should talk about simple life and daily events. He believes that true feelings can be shown best through the lives of ordinary people, especially those living in villages. So, he thinks poetry should focus on nature and natural surroundings.

He also explains what poetry is and what a poet does. Wordsworth says that simple rural life is closer to nature and truth. His poems are about “low and rustic life” and use the language of common people. This kind of language is easy to understand and can express deep human feelings. He also says that this style is better than the old Neo-Classical poetry, which was often complex and artificial.

In the Preface, Wordsworth supports a new kind of poetry that goes against old rules. He uses simple language because it connects easily with people’s hearts and minds. He believes poetry is for everyone, not just for rich or powerful people. Even though some critics did not agree with him, his ideas became very important. That is why _The Preface to The Lyrical Ballads_ is called the manifesto of Romantic poetry.
