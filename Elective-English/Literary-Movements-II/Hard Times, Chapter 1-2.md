Written by Charles Dickens, a British writer of the 19th century Victorian time, _Hard Times_ shows problems in the English school system of that time. The system was very strict and only cared about logic and rules, so it did not allow students to think freely or use imagination. The characters in these chapters show that when the human mind becomes too mechanical, it can harm the spirit and make people less human.

Chapter 1, _The One Thing Needful_, introduces us to Mr. Thomas Gradgrind, though his name is not told at first. Instead, he is described through his surroundings as very rigid and mechanical, with the word “square” used many times. This chapter shows that Mr. Gradgrind is strict and does not change his ideas. By repeating the word “facts,” it shows that he believes only facts and logic are important, and there is no place for imagination or creativity.

In Chapter 2, _Murdering The Innocents_, Gradgrind explains how to define a horse, which shows his strong focus on facts. He does not like that a girl named Cecilia Jupe is called “Sissy” and criticizes her father for giving her such a name. He asks her to define a horse, but she cannot answer. Then he asks a boy named Bitzer, who gives a perfect and memorized answer. This shows how mechanical the education system was. We also meet a government officer who, like Gradgrind, prefers “papering” instead of “painting” and likes facts more than imagination. At the end of the chapter, we meet Mr. M’Choakumchild, a teacher who has filled his mind with many subjects like grammar, language, science, math's, history, and more, but he teaches in a very mechanical and strict way.
