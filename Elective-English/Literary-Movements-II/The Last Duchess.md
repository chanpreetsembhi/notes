Written by Robert Browning, a poet from the Victorian era, The Last Duchess is a dark poem. It is told as a dramatic monologue. The poem shows a Duke who may have killed his wife, the Duchess. In the poem, we see his jealousy, pride, and need to control others. These traits may have led him to plan her death. After she died, he kept a painting of her to hide his bad actions from others.

In the poem, the Duke shows a visitor a painting of his dead wife. The painting was made by an artist named Fra Pandolf. He says the painting looks very real. As he speaks, he shares his complaints about her. He did not like her happy nature, her smiles, and her kindness. He calls these things foolish. This shows that he did not like her free and kind behavior and wanted her to act only as he wished. The line “I gave commands. Then all smiles stopped altogether.” suggests that he may have ordered her death.

The Duke also talks proudly about his name and family. He believes his name is very important and special. He thinks the Duchess did not respect his high status enough. For him, her simple joy in small things, like gifts or nature, was wrong. This shows that he cares more about pride and power than love. He wanted his wife to praise him only, not others or simple things.

Another important idea in the poem is control. The Duke controls the painting by keeping it behind a curtain. Only he can show it to others. This means he controls how people see the Duchess. When she was alive, he could not control her fully, but after her death, he feels he has full control over her image. This shows his strong desire to dominate everything around him.

At the end, the Duke points to a statue of Neptune taming a sea-horse. This shows that he wants to control his next wife too. He is already planning another marriage and speaks to the visitor about it. This makes him seem cold and unfeeling, because he moves on quickly after his wife’s death.

The poem shows how jealousy made the Duke treat his wife like an object, not a person. Her free spirit hurt his ego and may have led to her death. The poem also shows how men had power over women in that time. It warns us about the danger of pride, control, and lack of respect in relationships
