his poem is by Thomas Hardy, a writer and poet from the Realist time in English literature. The Man He Killed is a short poem where a soldier talks about killing another man in war. He thinks about it and feels it is strange and sad. He says that if he and the man he killed had met in a small pub before, they might have sat together and had drinks like good friends. But in war they were called enemies and had to fight. Hardy shows that people are made to hate each other by their leaders. So the soldier had to kill the man even if he did not really hate him. The poem shows that both men were just humans who could have been friends. It also shows that war is bad and hurts everyone. The poet also shows that leaders make people fight for their own reasons. The speaker feels confused and tries to explain why he did this, but he cannot find a good reason and feels unsure about it. He also thinks about how strange it is that two normal men meet as enemies because of war and orders from above, and this makes him feel very sad inside and quiet after it.

In the end, the soldier feels more sad and silent. He knows the man he killed was like him. Both were poor and maybe needed money, so they went to war. The soldier says it is “quaint and curious,” which means it is very strange. He cannot understand why they had to fight. This shows that war does not make sense. It also shows that people are not really enemies, but they are made enemies by war. The poem teaches us that peace is better than war and that all people are the same inside.
