## 1. Ballad

A Ballad is a story told in the form of a song by a singer or a group of singers. It is a short story written in a simple language. The Ballad is also an Important Part of Folk literature. Generally, It is sung by wandering singers The Ballads are of two types - Folk Ballads and Literary Ballads. Folk Ballads are written by unknown writers But literary Ballads are written by well known Poets and writers. **The Lady of Shalott by Tennyson** and **The Ballad of Father Gilligan by Yeats** are the examples of literary Ballads in English literature.

## 2. Lyric

A Lyric is a short poem and It is divided into stanzas. It describes the thoughts, feelings and emotions of the poet. It is very Personal in nature. The theme of the lyric is generally the poets own experiences or feelings. It is a kind of a song which can be sung and enjoyed. Moreover lyric is return in a simple and easy language. **The Daffodils by Wordsworth** is the best example of Lyric in English Language.

## 3. Ode

Ode is a long lyric Poem and It is generally serious in tone. It is written in the Praise of a person or some important event. The most famous writer of odes was **Greek Poet Pindar**. He wrote odes in the Honor of the winners of Olympic Games. In the modern time, ode to **The West Wind by Shelley** is a noted example of odes in English Literature.

## 4. Elegy

Elegy means a sad and mournful (sorrowful) song on the death of a person. it is very sad and serious in tone. So elegy is a poem which presents some tragic event like death and It brings tears in the eyes of the people. **"In memory of WB Yeats"** and **Elegy Written in a Country Churchyard** are the famous examples of Elegy in English literature.

## 5. Irony

Irony is a very important figure of speech in English language. It is a kind of language which has an inner as well as an outer meaning. The writers use irony in there works in order to create good impact. The novels of **Jane Austen** are the best examples of Irony in English literature. Moreover, Shakespeare has also used Irony in his plays (drama) like **Hamlet** and **Julius Caesar**.

## 6. Free Verse

Free verse means poetry without the quality of a regular meter. This type of poetry is generally written in short lines. The writers of free verse do not follow the old tradition of writing poetry. In England, **Arnold** and **Blake** contributed much to popularize the type of poetry. **Leaves of Grass by Whitman** is a famous example of free verse. free verse is very popular in these days because it gives much freedom to the writers to write poetry.

## 7. Dramatic Monologue

Mono means **one** and Logue means **language**. So it is a poem in which only one person speaks. The listener does not speak even a single word and remains a silent listener. It is presented in a dramatic style, that is why it is called a dramatic monologue. **Robert Browning** is the most famous writer of dramatic monologues. His famous dramatic monologues are **The Last Ride Together** and **My Last Duchess**. 

## 8. Blank Verse

Blank Verse means poetry without any rhyme it is called blank because the last words of lines do not rhyme. But blank Verse should not be compared with Free Verse because these two terms are very different. Blank Verse is mostly used him plays (dramas) and long poems. **The works of Shakespeare**, **Milton** and **Keats** are the best example of Blank Verse in English Literature.

## 9. Ambiguity

Ambiguity is a statement which has two or more meanings. This terms was popularized by **William Empson** in his book-**Seven Types of Ambiguity**. Sometimes the writer suggest different meanings for a situation in intentionally. This writers use this device element or a novel. So it is a statement which has different meanings. **Shakespeare** has also used Ambiguity in his famous play-**Antony and Cleopatra**.

## 10. Sonnet

A Poem of 14 lines is called a sonnet. In a sonnet, a single idea is introduction developed and concluded. A sonnet is of two types:-

##### The Shakespearean Sonnet
The Shakespearean sonnet has three stanzas of four lines and ending with a couplet. Shakespearean wrote 154 sonnets in English language.

##### The Petrarchan Sonnet 
The First eight lines of the Petrarchan sonnet are called as 'Octave' and the last six lines are
called as 'Sestet'. On his Blindness by Milton is the best example of Petrarchan sonnet in English literature.
